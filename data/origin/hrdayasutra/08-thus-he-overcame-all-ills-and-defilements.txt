# Thus, He Overcame All Ills and Defilements

All ills and defilements mean suffering. He perceived that all five skandhas are void, thereby transcending all suffering - included the suffering of Birth-and-Death allotment, the suffering of the Birth-an-Death realm's mortal changes.

According to the usual interpretation of the teachings, when it is fully understood that all five scandhas are empty, then the five fundamental conditions creating passion and delusion are severed, and two kinds of birth and death are finished. And the five fundamental conditions creating passion and delusion are clinging and attachments to: duality view, desire, form, formless realm, and state of delusion.

Everyone is equipped with the five skandhas, but those uninstructed in Buddhadharma cannot eradicate the five fundamental conditions and give rise to passion and delusion because they are unaware that they are originated by and dwell in the mind.

Such being the case, sentient beings have no other choice but to endure suffering in the present and turn endlessly in the cyclic pattern of existence until they recognize the cause of their suffering and enter the path to Enlightenment. To end the two kinds of Birth-and-Death and the five fundamental conditions, one must make a Great Vow to attain Enlightenment; And to be able to do that, one must study and practice the Buddhadharma.

At the time of his attainment of the Radiant Wisdom, the Bodhisattva Avalokitesvara conquered all ills and defilements by apprehending beyond any doublt that all five skandhas are devoid of independent existence.
